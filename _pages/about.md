I am a PhD Candidate in [Department of Technology Management & Innovation](https://engineering.nyu.edu/academics/departments/technology-management-and-innovation) at [NYU Tandon School of Engineering](https://engineering.nyu.edu/), working with [Dr. Graham Dove](https://engineering.nyu.edu/faculty/graham-dove). My research interests are at the intersection of Computer-Supported Collaborative Work (CSCW), Human-AI Interaction (HAI), and Geographic Information Science (GIScience).

I investigate how individuals and communities interact with geospatial technologies to make maps, and how GeoAI might be reshaping their interactions with these technologies, their collaborative dynamics, and the geographic information they produce. Through my research, I aim to produce empirical evidence that might inform the responsible design and integration of GeoAI systems.

At the individual level, my work focuses on how GeoAI shifts the roles, practices, and agency of people as they create, use, and communicate geospatial data, and (2) At the collective level, how GeoAI integration transforms the collaborative dynamics, participation structures, and governance mechanisms. 

That means I ask questions such as: 

- How do GIS and GeoAI tools shape participation and governance within open collaboration environments, such as OpenStreetMap?
- How are GeoAI tools shifting the roles, practices, and agency of people as they create, use, and communicate geospatial data
- How does the design of GeoAI tools influence specific human experiences and outcomes during specific tasks, such as geocoding?
- How does GeoAI affect human knowledge about geographic phenomena?

Geographic knowledge is collaboratively produced within sociotechnical systems where the social and technical subsystems are inextricably linked and interdependent. My work will aim to understand how social choices shape technical outcomes and technical choices shape social outcomes. Humans and technologies mutually shape each other. My work will explore how geospatial technologies shape users’ perception, understanding, and navigation of the world, and how users’ values and collaborative dynamics shape these technologies.

Prior to my PhD, I spent 6+ years in urban strategy consulting and community development. I leveraged geospatial analytics to support public, private, and nonprofit clients/partners across the Northeast US on projects ranging from renewable energy and infrastructure planning to environmental justice and economic development.
